Lake Access

On this page you will find all the information about lake access and all information about accessing the lake

Here is a summary

This lake does have motorized boats but discourages PWC (personal watercraft). There is a municipal by-law in place that insists that all watercraft be washed and receive a certificate to that effect from one of the three boat wash stations installed by the municipality.

All boats(boats motorized and not, canoes, Kayaks, pedalos, stand up paddle boards etc. Must have a municipality identification sticker. Please contact the municipality to obtain a sticker. https://wentworth-nord.ca

Municipal Boat Wash

There is a barrier installed by the municipality to keep motorized vehicles off the Chemin de Fer Road. You would need access to this road to get to the boat launch ramp. Stephen Yeats has the required key (phone: 450-327-6277 or info.lsfx@gmail.com) The standard is 24 hours before launch but some exceptions can be made.

Once at the boat ramp, you would need to use the key to unlock the chain that controls access to the lake. A member of the lake association executive will provide that key to unlock the chain and allow your boat access but not a PWC (personal watercraft) provided that:

  1

Your boat has been washed and you have a certificate to that effect.

  2

You have a legitimate place to park your vehicle and trailer (note that all the parking in the area is private as residents have had to pay for the spaces they use.

  3

You have a place to moor your boat - note that we have no marina or public dock.

These may seem like harsh measures but we are very concerned about the spread of invasive plant species, particularly Eurasian Milfoil, which has infested several lakes in the area to the extent that it has significantly and negatively impacted the use and enjoyment of those lakes.

Boating Safety

Forth

  • The wake from a boat can result in damage to docks, the shoreline and also to other boats. It can also be a risk for swimmers, divers and people in small boats that might capsize. Therefore please consider how your boat’s wake might affect others when selecting your speed.

  • Speed limits are posted at the narrows/slow zones and by bridges. These are slow, no wake zones and speed should never exceed 10kph or 7mph. Note that you are responsible for any harm or damages you may cause.

  • All watercraft must have lights switched on between dusk and sunrise. This is federal law.

  • All swimmers should stay close to the shoreline, be visible and should never swim alone. Children and weak swimmers should always wear a life jacket when near, on, or in the water for waterskiing and other recreational towing activities (This is federal law).

Boating Rules for Towing

- You must have a spotter on board the boat, who will keep watch on each person you are towing and can communicate directly and easily with the driver of the boat.
- There must be an unoccupied seat on the towboat for each person being towed in the event that they need to come on board.
- Towing is not permitted when there is poor visibility
- It is good practice to keep sports that involve towing in the centre of the lake and to not spend too much time in one place.
